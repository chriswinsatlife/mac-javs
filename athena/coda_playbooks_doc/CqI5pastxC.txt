Playbook Design

Background



Purpose

The purpose of the playbook document is to:

* Get the client excited about something they can delegate or automate
* Make it clear what other top delegators are doing in an area where they might have low visibility
* Help them understand the value of turning on this particular delegation
* Provide clear instructions to the client on what they have to do
* Provide clear instructions to the EA on what they have to do
* Segment the instructions into the setup and the ongoing workflows

Playbook Strategy



Context

When considering and prioritizing playbooks, we might assess their benefits in a variety of areas. Depending on near-term business priorities, the playbook team could focus on a single area vs. aiming for a portfolio approach.

Benefits

Playbooks can serve different roles, including:

* Activation
  * When playbooks enable quicker time-to-benefit for new clients, or create infrastructure that enables more frequent or more proactive delegation, weâ€™ll be less likely to see churn in M1-M3
* Retention
  * Fairly obvious; the more a playbook enables repeat delegation or a greater surface area of business/life delegation, the greater dependency on the Athena platform
* Revenue Expansion
  * Playbooks emphasizing multiplayer use throughout the organization can lead to more seats/EAs, as can super valuable playbooks that ramp up EA time and trigger plan upgrades
  * A playbook can be the first foray or test MVP around a potentially new service offering, like chief of staff or highly-specialized EAs (e.g. venture capital analyst)
* Growth
  * Referrals
    * Certain playbooks are likely to have a strong word-of-mouth factor, especially those involving external relationships
  * Virality
    * Many playbooks can be made into cornerstone content for public content marketing, e.g. CRM
    * Partnering with clients to help formalize/share their playbooks can be a strong way to juice referrals (e.g. if they share the playbook we polished/expanded with them via social)
    * Playbook-like content, e.g. podcast interviews with Athena clients, can serve a similar purpose even in the absence of an associated playbook
    * Partnering on content with tools used in playbooks, especially less popular ones, can be a source of traffic (e.g. work on a company blog post for Clay, Descript, etc)
    * Similar idea applies for venture firms (e.g. First Round Capitalâ€™s posts with Sam Corcos) or media platforms around productivity, management, startups, etc
