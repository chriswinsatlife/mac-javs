# Comprehensive Directory of Personal Systems, Routines, and Setup Catalogs

### Executive Summary
This report provides an exhaustive catalog of websites, communities, and databases that document the personal habits, workflows, and equipment of individuals across various industries. These resources are categorized into ten distinct verticals, ranging from software tool stacks and developer dotfiles to daily routines and physical carry gear. For competitive SEO research, these platforms represent high-value targets for understanding user intent, identifying affiliate opportunities, and analyzing content structures that successfully aggregate niche personal data. The list includes established directories, niche blogs, and community-driven repositories such as subreddits and GitHub archives.

---

## 1. Tool Stacks & Software Setups
This category focuses on the specific software combinations (stacks) professionals use to build products or manage their digital lives. These sites often feature "uses" pages or database-style listings of SaaS tools.

*   **Uses.tech**
    A massive directory of developer "uses" pages. It aggregates links to personal websites where developers detail their hardware, software, and desk setups. It is a goldmine for finding individual "stack" pages [cite: 1].

*   **StackShare**
    A leading platform where companies and developers list the full technology stacks they use (e.g., "Airbnb uses React, Nginx, etc."). It allows for comparisons and discovery of tools used by successful engineering teams [cite: 2].

*   **Startup Stash**
    A curated directory of tools and resources for startups and entrepreneurs. It categorizes software by function (e.g., CRM, Design, Analytics), effectively acting as a "stack" recommendation engine for founders [cite: 3].

*   **Toolfinder.co**
    As noted in the query, this site catalogs productivity tools and software stacks, often featuring reviews and "best of" lists for personal productivity setups [cite: 4, 5].

*   **BuiltWith**
    While automated rather than self-reported, this tool indexes the technology stacks of millions of websites. It is essential for researching what successful competitors are running "under the hood" [cite: 6].

*   **Alternative.me**
    A crowdsourced platform that lists software alternatives. While not a "stack" site per se, it catalogs user preferences by showing what tools people are switching *to* and *from*, revealing workflow shifts [cite: 7].

*   **Indie Hackers Stacks**
    A directory within the Indie Hackers community where founders share the exact software tools they use to build and run their businesses. It provides a transparent look at the low-cost stacks of bootstrapped startups [cite: 8].

## 2. Daily Routines & Habits
These websites focus on the behavioral protocols, schedules, and habits of successful individuals, often breaking down their days hour-by-hour.

*   **Routines.club**
    A database of daily routines from influential figures like Elon Musk and Andrew Huberman. It breaks down their days into specific activities (sleep, deep work, exercise) and often links to the products they use [cite: 9].

*   **My Morning Routine**
    An interview-based site dedicated exclusively to how people spend their mornings. It features hundreds of interviews with creatives and entrepreneurs about their waking habits, breakfast choices, and early-hour workflows [cite: 10, 11].

*   **Zen Habits**
    While primarily a blog, it serves as a repository of habit-formation protocols. It is frequently cited in productivity communities as a foundational resource for minimalist routine building [cite: 12].

*   **JamesClear.com**
    The author of *Atomic Habits* maintains a repository of articles and interviews that deconstruct habit formation. His content often features case studies of famous individuals' routines [cite: 13].

*   **Daily Routines (Blog)**
    Often referenced in productivity circles, this niche site (and similar archives) catalogs the historical daily schedules of famous writers, artists, and scientists (e.g., Kafka, Beethoven).

*   **r/getdisciplined & r/productivity**
    These subreddits act as dynamic, community-driven databases of personal routines. Users frequently post their own "daily protocols" for critique and optimization, offering real-world data on habit stacking [cite: 12, 14].

## 3. Desk Setups, Workspaces & Gear
Visual-heavy directories that showcase home office and workstation setups, often with annotated product links.

*   **Minimal Desk Setups**
    A curated gallery of minimalist workspaces. It focuses on clean aesthetics and lists the specific monitors, desks, and peripherals used in each photo [cite: 15, 16].

*   **Desk Setups (desksetups.net)**
    A platform allowing users to upload their setups. It features annotated images where hovering over an item (like a keyboard or mouse) reveals the product name and price [cite: 17].

*   **Maker Stations**
    Features high-quality tours of home offices belonging to makers and creatives. It goes beyond photos to include interviews about how the physical space influences their productivity [cite: 18].

*   **Workspaces.xyz**
    A newsletter and website archive that profiles the desk setups of designers and developers. It is known for high-resolution photography and detailed gear lists [cite: 19].

*   **Desk Hunt**
    A reader-submission site where users share their workspace configurations. It includes interviews with the owners about their design choices and equipment [cite: 20].

*   **Office Snapshots**
    While focused on corporate offices, this site catalogs the interior design and furniture "stacks" of major tech companies, offering inspiration for professional-grade setups [cite: 21].

*   **r/battlestations**
    A massive subreddit where PC gamers and power users post photos of their high-performance desk setups. It is a primary source for trends in lighting, multi-monitor arrays, and cable management [cite: 15].

## 4. Workout Routines of Specific People
Databases that reverse-engineer or republish the exercise programs of celebrities, athletes, and fictional characters.

*   **Superhero Jacked**
    A comprehensive database of workout routines inspired by celebrities (e.g., Henry Cavill, The Rock) and fictional characters. It includes detailed set/rep schemes and diet plans [cite: 22].

*   **Muscle & Strength (Celebrity Workouts)**
    A major fitness site with a dedicated section for celebrity workout plans. It provides downloadable PDFs and expert breakdowns of stars' training regimens [cite: 23, 24].

*   **Superhuman Fitness**
    Similar to Superhero Jacked, this site analyzes and lists the body-sculpting routines of Hollywood actors, often including video breakdowns of the exercises [cite: 25].

*   **Jacked Gorilla**
    A popular niche site that lists the workout routines and diet plans of bodybuilders, athletes, and celebrities. (Note: Often appears in search results alongside the above).

*   **r/Fitness & r/bodyweightfitness**
    Community wikis and threads within these subreddits often catalog specific "famous" routines (like the Recommended Routine or 5/3/1) and user modifications of celebrity plans [cite: 26].

## 5. Diet & Nutrition Protocols
Sites that track what people eat, ranging from celebrity diet plans to "food diaries" of regular people.

*   **Superhero Jacked (Celeb Diet Database)**
    In addition to workouts, this site maintains a specific database for celebrity diet plans, detailing macronutrient splits and specific food choices of actors preparing for roles [cite: 27].

*   **People.com (Food Diaries)**
    The "Food Diaries" vertical features interviews with celebrities where they detail exactly what they eat in a day, often accompanied by photos of their meals [cite: 28].

*   **The Christian Nutritionist**
    An example of a niche expert blog that publishes "What I Eat in a Day" posts, detailing specific dietary protocols (e.g., gluten-free, whole foods) from a personal perspective [cite: 29].

*   **MyFoodDiary**
    While primarily a tracking tool, its public forums and success stories often feature detailed logs of users' nutritional habits and daily food intake [cite: 30].

*   **FitBudd**
    Publishes analyses of famous celebrity diet charts, breaking down meal-by-meal examples for figures like Kim Kardashian [cite: 31].

*   **"What I Eat in a Day" Blogs**
    Various lifestyle blogs serve as individual catalogs of eating habits. Examples include *Dished by Denise* (Slimming World protocols) [cite: 32] and *A Lady Goes West* [cite: 33].

## 6. Creator Workflows & Content Creation Setups
Resources dedicated to the cameras, microphones, lighting, and software used by YouTubers, podcasters, and streamers.

*   **Kit.co**
    A massive platform where creators curate "kits" of the gear they use. It is the standard for finding "YouTuber gear lists" and "Podcasting setups" [cite: 34, 35].

*   **Shotkit**
    Specifically for photographers, this site features "Inside My Camera Bag" posts where professionals lay out every lens, body, and accessory they carry [cite: 36].

*   **The Podcasters' Studio (Gear)**
    A directory of podcasting equipment recommendations and setups used by professional audio creators [cite: 37].

*   **CreatorStack**
    A directory of tools and resources specifically for the creator economy, listing the software stacks used by influencers to monetize and manage their businesses [cite: 38].

*   **StreamingEquip**
    A database tracking the equipment (mice, keyboards, headsets) used by professional streamers and e-sports players [cite: 39].

*   **Individual Creator "Gear" Pages**
    Many creators maintain dedicated pages on their personal sites. Examples include *Smiti Nathan's* YouTube Gear List [cite: 40] and *Larry G's* Ultimate Gear List [cite: 41].

## 7. Developer Setups & Dotfiles
Technical repositories where programmers share their configuration files (dotfiles) and development environments.

*   **Dotfiles.github.io**
    GitHub's official guide and collection of dotfiles. It links to popular repositories where developers share their shell configurations, editor themes, and tool settings [cite: 42].

*   **Awesome Dotfiles (GitHub)**
    A curated list of dotfile repositories, frameworks, and tools. It categorizes setups by operating system and shell (Zsh, Bash, Fish) [cite: 43].

*   **Uses.tech (Developer Section)**
    While listed in Tool Stacks, this site is primarily populated by developers sharing their IDEs, terminals, and coding fonts [cite: 1].

*   **Dev.to**
    A community platform where developers frequently write articles detailing their "Ultimate Development Setup" or "2024 Mac Setup," often including code snippets and config files [cite: 44].

*   **MacStories Shortcuts Archive**
    A comprehensive directory of Apple Shortcuts for iOS and macOS automation. While not "dotfiles" in the traditional sense, these serve as the "code" for mobile workflows [cite: 45].

## 8. Entrepreneur & Founder Stacks
Directories that reveal the operational software and business tools used by startup founders.

*   **Indie Hackers (Products/Stacks)**
    The "Products" section of Indie Hackers allows founders to list the tech stack and revenue generation tools behind their businesses [cite: 8].

*   **Startup Stash**
    As mentioned, this is a primary directory for finding the tools entrepreneurs use to build their MVPs and manage operations [cite: 3].

*   **Product Hunt**
    Product launches often include a "Maker" comment or section detailing the stack used to build the product. The platform itself serves as a discovery engine for new tools in founder stacks [cite: 2, 46].

*   **Founder Stacks (StartStacked)**
    Niche sites like *StartStacked* provide curated lists of tools recommended for launching digital businesses quickly [cite: 47].

*   **StackShare (Startup Section)**
    Specifically filters for "Unicorns" or "Startups" to see what high-growth companies are using [cite: 2].

## 9. 'What's In My Bag' & EDC (Everyday Carry)
Sites dedicated to the items people carry daily, from pocket knives and wallets to tech accessories.

*   **EverydayCarry.com**
    The largest database of "pocket dumps." Users upload photos of their daily gear, and the site tags each item for purchase. It covers everything from tactical gear to office essentials [cite: 48].

*   **Carryology**
    A high-end blog and community focused on "better ways to carry." It features reviews and interviews about bags, wallets, and travel gear [cite: 49].

*   **Pack Config**
    A site dedicated to how people pack their bags. It features "loadouts" for different scenarios (travel, hiking, commuting) [cite: 50].

*   **Amoeba Music "What's In My Bag"**
    A famous video series and directory where musicians and artists show off the records and movies they bought. It’s a cultural "bag" check rather than utility gear [cite: 51].

*   **r/EDC**
    A massive subreddit where users post daily photos of their pocket contents. It is a primary source for trends in the EDC community [cite: 52, 53].

*   **r/whatsinmybag**
    Similar to EDC but often focuses more on bags/purses and their total contents rather than just pocket items [cite: 54].

## 10. Interview-Based 'How I Work' Content
Platforms that interview professionals specifically about their workflows, productivity hacks, and systems.

*   **Lifehacker "How I Work"**
    A long-running series interviewing productive people about their workspaces, shortcuts, and routines. It is one of the most extensive archives of this type of content [cite: 55, 56].

*   **Uses This (usesthis.com)**
    A minimalist interview site where people from all walks of life answer two questions: "Who are you?" and "What do you use?" (hardware and software) [cite: 57, 58].

*   **Remote Habits**
    Features interviews with remote workers about their daily routines, desk setups, and productivity tips for working from home [cite: 59, 60].

*   **Balance the Grind**
    (Implied context from research on routine sites) A site dedicated to interviews about work-life balance, daily schedules, and productivity habits.

*   **The Creative Independent**
    Publishes interviews with artists and writers that often delve deeply into their creative processes, daily rituals, and emotional workflows.
